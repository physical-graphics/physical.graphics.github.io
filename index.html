TJRE - Realistic Modelling Tool in Catia & Blender

NURBs & Sub-D developed to ISO 4000-1 based on 42 real tyres

  • ISO 4000-1 and ETRTO definitions give only simplistic volumes for packaging and visualisation.
  • This carefully-reasearched modelling tool generates dimensionally- and visually-accurate tyre models.

Augmented Reality Invisible A-Pillar

Embedded C++ development with eye tracking to reveal hazards

  • The pillars either side of the windshield block a significant part of the driver's view.
  • This solution uses ray tracing from the tracked driver's eyes to crop and transform the feed from an external camera.
  • Running at 45Hz with a bespoke eye-tracking method for robustness, this felt really wonderful when driving.

Procedural Reproduction of Real Landscapes

Multiple GIS sources upsampled with bespoke Python tooling

  • DEFRA aerial LiDAR scan (1m res.) and colour satellite imagery (0.1m res.) are combined and aligned by georeferencing.
  • Additional geometry detail is inferred by image processing of height field together with colour imagery.
  • Automated Python pipeline outputs UV-mapped mesh tiles and detailed heightmap tiles for visual and physics use.

Simulation of Mirror-Replacement Display

Visualisation of performance for ECE R45 and ISO 16505-2

  • Homologation requirements are calculated in video frame by reverse-engineering and construction in Catia.
  • Synchronised video feeds are transformed into correct relationship with CAD capture of interior to form cohesive view for evaluation.
  • This example is a reference reconstruction of a prior car in market. Project reviews included adapting this data for VR viewing.
